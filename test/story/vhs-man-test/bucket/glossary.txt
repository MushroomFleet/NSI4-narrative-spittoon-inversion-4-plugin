Backrooms: Infinite, non-Euclidean maze of yellow-tinted office spaces, a liminal void where reality noclips.
Noclip: To glitch out of normal reality, falling through floors or walls into the Backrooms.
VHS-MAN: Spectral entity made of tangled VHS tape, bipedal with glowing blue 80s LED eyes, chaotic benevolent traverser of realms via doors.
Ash Harlan: Protagonist, janitor at dying video store, wisecracking survivor modeled after Ash Williams from Evil Dead.
Analog Liminality: The universe's core mechanic where everyday doors become unstable portals to the Backrooms.
Media Manifestations: Malevolent entities born from discarded analog media, like Betamax hulks or film-reel tendrils, haunting the Backrooms.
Infinite Rental: The story's title, symbolizing Ash's looping ordeals in the analog limbo.
Groovy: Ash's signature quip, invoking 80s nostalgia amid horror.
Obsolescence Horror: Theme of forgotten tech (VHS, cassettes) coming alive as vengeful forces in the digital age.
Looping Nostalgia: The repeating twist where Ash's Backrooms trips are cyclical, trapping him in eternal analog past.
